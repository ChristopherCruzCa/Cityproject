Isaac Albéniz.

Isaac Manuel Francisco Albéniz y Pascual (Camprodón, Gerona, May 29, 1860-Cambo-les-Bains, May 18, 1909) was a famous Spanish composer and pianist, a disciple of Felipe Pedrell. Thanks to the help of William Morphy, the Count of Morphy, composer and patron of the arts, he obtained a pension to train at the Brussels Conservatory in 1876, and finished his studies in 1879. Being a virtuoso piano composer, Isaac Albéniz also composed music for other instruments. He dedicated more than a decade of his almost forty-nine years of life to writing themes for the theater —such as, for example, his most successful operatic work, Pepita Jiménez— he also dedicated himself to performing concerts or recording his opera Merlín. During his career he wrote songs—more than two dozen—as well as various orchestral and chamber themes. Isaac Albéniz's personal collection is preserved in the Library of Catalonia and in the Music Museum of Barcelona, ​​which keeps the donation offered by the composer's granddaughter (includes biographical documentation, original manuscript copies and personal objects). There is an "Albéniz Foundation", with offices in Madrid and Santander, currently chaired by Paloma O'Shea.

Pol Salvador.

A native of Camprodón, Gerona, Pol Salvador is a player trained in the base categories of Villarreal CF, RCD Español and CE Mataró. In 2018 he signed for UD Las Palmas to play in the youth team. On February 26, 2019, as a player of the División de Honor Juvenil, he renewed his contract with UD Las Palmas for three seasons.1 On April 19, 2019, Salvador made his debut for Unión Deportiva Las Palmas "C" in the Tercera División de España, in a goalless draw as against CD El Cotillo. On July 20, 2020, he made his debut with the UD Las Palmas first team in the Second Division, in a match against Extremadura UD that would end with a victory by five goals to one. In said match the midfielder would play 25 minutes and he would suffer a fortuitous blow with the goalkeeper, Álex Domínguez, which would cause him a slight head injury, for which he would end up in the hospital. During the 2020-21 season, he would be part of Las Palmas Atlético in the Second Division B and would be part of the calls and training sessions for the UD Las Palmas first team in the Second Division. During the 2021-22 season, he would be part of Las Palmas Atlético of the second RFEF and would also be part of the UD Las Palmas first team calls in the second division, having played a total of three games, one of the league in the Gran Canaria stadium on December 5, 2021 against Sporting de Gijón that would end with the Canarian victory by a goal to nil and played two other games in the Copa del Rey, the first of them on December 2, 2021 against Vélez CF and with victory of the Canarian team by two goals to three and the second game he played was on December 15, 2021 against Real Valladolid at the José Zorrilla stadium in which he made his debut as a starter and having played a total of 58 minutes, in which the local team won by three goals to one and with this result ended up eliminating the Canarian team from the Copa del Rey.
